Short Bio

I received the B.Eng. (Honors) degree in Electronic Engineering and M.Sc. degree in Circuits and Systems, in 2018 and 2021, respectively, both from Fudan University, China, advised by Prof. Wei Chen and Dr. Chenyun Dai. During my master stage, I also collaborated closely with Prof. Ted Clancy. My master research projects mainly include medical artificial intelligence, human-machine interface, physiological computing and biomedical signal processing (EMG signals in particular) with applications to various industrial and clinical scenarios.

In this autumn (2021), I will join Prof. Dario Farina's Lab at Imperial College London to pursue my PhD degree. My PhD research will focus on deep learning and machine intelligence techniques applied to invasive EMG sensing, to decode the motion intention of amputees for intuitive control of prostheses and exoskeletons. My long goal is to help amputees re-gain their athletic abilities by developing next generation intelligent exoskeletons.
